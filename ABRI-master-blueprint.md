0. WHAT ABRI ACTUALLY IS
   ABRI is the verified business network for Malaysia — trust infrastructure that lets real, SSM-verified companies prove they are legitimate, vouch for one another, and find counterparties they can transact with safely. It is not a digital business card, not a directory, not a LinkedIn replacement. It wins by occupying the one quadrant no incumbent holds — high verification × high network effect — inside one dense Klang Valley professional-services cluster first, then replicating the cluster playbook across Malaysia and ASEAN. Its moat is the defended vouching graph + cluster density + owner-verified data, compounding toward becoming the trusted, machine-readable supply layer that human buyers and AI procurement agents rely on.

1. WHY NOW (the tailwind — was missing from v4)
   Two forces compound:
   (a) Trust collapse. AI is flooding every open platform with fakes — LinkedIn removed 100M+ fake accounts in 2024; ~10.7% of Google reviews are fake (240M+ removed in 2024). The open networks are becoming less trustworthy every year.
   (b) Agentic commerce. AI procurement agents (Alibaba's Accio already at 10M+ MAU) increasingly mediate B2B buying, and they need verifiable, structured trust signals to choose suppliers. A verified, vouched, machine-readable business graph becomes more valuable every year.
   The positive frame: verifiability is becoming a currency. ABRI lets a business hold that currency from day one — and, later, exposes it as the agent-readable supply layer AI buyers read from.

THE FOUNDING PRINCIPLES

1. Trust is the product; the network is the moat; NFC is the distribution wedge. Never position or price ABRI as a card app or directory.

2. Density before breadth. Seed to ≥60–70% of the real corridor firm-count (pull it from SSM/MyData — never assume) before opening a segment.

3. The friction is the moat. When verifying, curating, vouching, or recruiting the first 100 feels troublesome, the troublesome part is the differentiation. Don't remove it.

4. Stage features, never stage trust. The verified promise is present from minute one; the features fulfilling it roll out over time.

5. Each trust layer gates the next. Can't vouch unless you're a Verified Representative; can't review unless you transacted. This is what makes ABRI structurally un-copyable.

6. Build the vouching graph; buy everything commodity. SSM data, email/phone/IP/device signals, eKYC, passkeys, payments — all bought. Only the graph is built.

7. Give-first: make giving into the network easier than extracting from it. Vouch, thank, introduce — status and leads flow back as a consequence.

8. Lead with credibility and status, never fear. The scam-check is discovered, not sold.

THE CARD — A PHYSICAL TRUST TOKEN
Verification status renders before contact details on every tap.

Member taps verified member → live SSM badge + vouch count + "member since" first. Actions: exchange verified profiles + vouch.
Member taps someone not on ABRI → honest flag: "Not yet verified on ABRI." The scam-check, discovered not sold. Action: invite to verify.
Non-member taps a member's card → member looks credible; visitor pulled in: "Create your verified profile." Every tap recruits.
After any tap → WhatsApp: "Someone viewed your verified profile near [location]." Passive lead capture.

Pricing model (corrected): free for the founding 100; thereafter a separate paid item, not bundled into the subscription. Replacement cards for existing members ~RM50. Hardware is modelled as a low-margin pass-through / acquisition cost — never folded into ARPU.

THE GIVE-FIRST ENGINE
Give actions (what members DO): Vouch (+ optional 140-char testimonial) · Thank · Vouch back · Exchange profiles · Introduce two people (Stage 2).
Receive back (flows automatically): vouch count grows · testimonials displayed · tier + ladder badges unlock · profile views rise · search ranking improves (Stage 3).
Design test for every feature: does this make giving easier, or extracting easier? Build the former; deprioritise the latter. (Bartlett: "if you try to extract from the network, it collapses.")
The reciprocity loop — vouch → notify → thank → vouch back — activates two members per cycle.

THE STAGED ROLLOUT
STAGE 0 — SEEDED LAUNCH (in MVP)
Claimable listing pages from public registry + website data only (no personal data), labelled "Unverified — seeded from public data" (T0). Basic search. Seed to ≥60–70% of the real corridor firm-count — pulled from SSM/MyData in Days 1–30, never an assumed number.
STAGE 1 — VERIFIED CORE + SIMPLE VOUCHING (in MVP)

Claim flow; profiles; all four NFC tap scenarios; email/phone MFA; admin approval queue; WhatsApp digest.
Simple vouch button: capped, identity-gated, manually reviewed (feasible at N=100–600; this is the whole point of the hybrid call — strongest referral mechanic without over-building).
SSM verification manual at MVP (free e-Info lookup + optional RM15.40 profile for director-match). T1/T2 badges.
Founding 100: free year 1, free card, founding badge.
Pricing: RM490/yr standard, RM1,490/yr business (10 profiles).

Stage 1 gates: ≥100 founding members · ≥60% verified · ≥3 partners → then K ≥ 0.3 (Q2 gate) · ≥40% activated → then ≥250 paying, CAC ≤RM150, ≤3%/month cancellation (Q3 gate).
STAGE 2 — SOPHISTICATED TRUST LAYER + RELATIONSHIP (post-MVP fast-follow)

Introduce two people (needs N=300+ for a meaningful intro pool). Both notified; introducer's profile shows "Made 12 introductions."
EigenTrust/SybilRank trust propagation; vouch budgets + decay; automated ring detection (the graph analytics moat — built now that density warrants it).
Embeddable verified-badge widget (website/email/LinkedIn — organic distribution + switching cost).
Optional eKYC (T3); passkeys/WebAuthn.
Vouch-driven invites; Network Leader tier.

STAGE 3 — DISCOVERY (post-MVP)
Trust-ranked search (verification + vouch activity, not ad spend); RFP/supplier matching; needs board; warm-path intros; real-time SSM API. Density-dependent — only viable at 1,000+.
STAGE 4 — TRANSACTIONS (post-MVP)
RFP workflow, milestones, invoicing; transaction-gated two-sided blind reviews (T4); escrow via a BNM-licensed partner (iPay88/Razer/Curlec) — never build payments, never hold funds; MyInvois integration. Take-rate 2–2.5%.
